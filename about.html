About Me

The scientist
behind the work

I'm a neuroimaging scientist who has spent 15+ years building the pipelines and analysis infrastructure that turn raw MRI data into publishable science — across environmental health, clinical, and neurotrauma research.

01

Background

My career in neuroscience began at Bowling Green State University, where I completed a dual degree in Psychology & Neuroscience and Creative Writing & English — a combination that has quietly shaped how I communicate science ever since. I went on to earn my Ph.D. in Neuroscience from the University of Cincinnati, where my dissertation focused on the lasting effects of childhood lead exposure on adult brain structure using multimodal MRI.

What drew me to neuroimaging wasn't just the technology — it was the idea that you could hold a brain scan and see the fingerprints of someone's environment, their history, their exposures. That conviction has carried through every project I've worked on, from childhood air pollution cohorts to sports concussion research.

Over the years I've built and maintained analysis pipelines for three concurrent NIH-funded longitudinal studies, trained graduate students, reviewed manuscripts for journals ranging from NeuroImage to The Lancet Planetary Health, and contributed to over a dozen conference presentations. More recently, I've been applying that scientific grounding to AI model development — helping build better reasoning in large language models by bringing domain expertise from neuroscience and biomedical research.

02

Approach to Research

Rigor & Reproducibility

I build pipelines with documentation, QC checkpoints, and version control from the start — not as an afterthought.

Methodological Breadth

Working across fMRI, DTI, MRS, and volumetric analysis has made me a generalist who can speak fluently across imaging domains.

Translational Focus

The goal is always to connect technical pipeline work to real scientific questions — and to communicate those findings clearly.

03

Outside the Lab

My B.F.A. in Creative Writing isn't just a footnote — writing has always been how I process and communicate ideas. Whether drafting a methods section, a manuscript, or a grant narrative, I care about clarity and precision in language as much as in analysis.

I'm based in Bloomington, Indiana, home to Indiana University and a vibrant research community I'm glad to be part of.
